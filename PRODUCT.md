# Prepora

## Product definition

Prepora is a web workspace for students and early-career job seekers preparing for interviews and applications. It brings interview practice, coaching, resume creation, and resume review into one focused product.

## Positioning

The product turns practice and feedback into a clear next action. It should feel like a steady coach: prepared, specific, and encouraging without being theatrical.

## Primary users

- Students preparing for internships or graduate roles
- Early-career candidates building confidence and practical interview skills
- Job seekers improving a resume for a specific application

## Core jobs

1. Practice a realistic interview.
2. Understand what went well and what to improve next.
3. Build a clear, credible resume.
4. Review a resume against role expectations.
5. Return later and immediately know where to continue.

## Product personality

- Calm, never cold
- Professional, never corporate theater
- Encouraging, never congratulatory by default
- Direct, never abrupt
- Evidence-led, never decorative

## Experience principles

1. **One clear next step.** Every screen should make the primary action obvious.
2. **Progress stays visible.** Users should understand what is complete, what is active, and what comes next.
3. **Evidence over spectacle.** Feedback, examples, and outcomes carry the interface; decoration does not.
4. **A workspace, not a marketing template.** Authenticated pages prioritize tasks and information over slogans.
5. **Support under pressure.** Language, hierarchy, and interaction patterns remain legible when a user is anxious or hurried.
6. **Honest system states.** Loading, empty, error, and success states explain what happened and what the user can do.

## Accessibility baseline

- WCAG 2.2 AA contrast targets
- Complete keyboard operation and visible focus treatment
- Reduced-motion support
- Status is never communicated by color alone
- Form labels and errors remain associated and understandable
- Responsive navigation remains available at every supported width

## Explicit anti-patterns

- Purple or neon AI branding
- Gradients, glows, glass panels, floating blobs, or robot mascots
- Dense walls of interchangeable cards
- Empty motivational copy without a useful action
- Decorative progress or fabricated metrics
- Excessive pills, badges, rounded containers, or shadow elevation
- Raw provider errors or implementation language in user-facing messages
